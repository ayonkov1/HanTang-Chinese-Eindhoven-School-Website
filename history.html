It all began in 1978

In 1978 the Eindhoven Chinese School was founded by the Association of Eindhoven Chinese Protestants in Eindhoven. At that time there were only 20 students who learned Cantonese from Hong Kong textbooks every Saturday in a café on the Grote Berg.

Changes during the 80s and 90s

In 1986 the number of pupils grew to 120. At that time, the Association of Chinese Protestants in Eindhoven thought it was more practical to share responsibility with the Chinese Association in Eindhoven. The Chinese school also approached the municipality for help. That was initially refused, but the actions of the students under the direction of the school brought the municipal administration to a different mind. In 1987 we were allowed to use the Genderdal public school for free, but unfortunately we had to move in November 1989 when the municipality sold the buildings. After that, the organization of the school changed in a number of steps. Since 1993, the school has been supervised by the Eindhoven Chinese School Foundation, which was then established. The Foundation monitors education as well as finances.

About the language

Due to the growing international contacts of China, the commonly spoken Mandarin language is becoming more popular. This language also takes over the role of the Cantonese dialect. (Mandarin and all dialects use the same Chinese characters, only the pronunciation differs. Our school now has about 400 students, both children of Chinese-speaking parents and Dutch-speaking adults, but also Dutch-speaking children, including adopted Chinese children with an interest in their roots. The classes today are focused on language proficiency, mainly in the standard language of China. The students who have already started with Cantonese and Mandarin in Taiwanese style can complete their course cycle (Taiwanese Mandarin has the same pronunciation as mainland Mandarin).

Community

The role of the Eindhoven Chinese School in the cultural life of the Chinese community in the Eindhoven area is very important. For example, the Association of Chinese Elderly, the Chinese Women's Group Eindhoven, the Chinese Sports Association, the Chinese Dance School of Li Feng, use our location Eckart College for all kinds of Saturday activities. We also have hobby classes for Chinese calligraphy, Chinese dance and the martial arts Gongfu (often written as Kung Fu). These are intended for those who want to get in touch with Chinese culture half an hour after the language lessons.

Origin of the name

In 2007 - 2008 we expanded the name of the school (the Foundation itself does not change its name). Our school is now called Eindhoven Chinese School HanTang. Anyone who knows anything about Chinese history also knows that the Han (206 BC - 220 AD) and Tang (618-907) dynasties had a brilliant culture. The beautiful name HanTang is also an incentive for ourselves to achieve more in the cultural field.

30th Anniversary of the school

One of our biggest and most memorable events was the celebration of our school's 30th anniversary and China Day on April 26, 2008. On that day, there were 19 performances about Chinese music, dancing, kung fu, lectures about the Olympics and history and culture, also cozy stalls. That was a lot of work for all teachers, students and also musicians inside and outside the school.

40 years later

Our greatest achievement was when our school was chosen as a 'Model School for Learning Chinese' by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs in China, Overseas Chinese Department.
