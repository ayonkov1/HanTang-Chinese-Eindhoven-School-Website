ONLINE INTRAMURAL CHESS TOURNAMENT

How does one get to hang out with classmates on a Saturday night with restrictions to go outside? Given how many students are no strangers to multiplayer online competitions, while being avid chess enthusiasts, there is only one solution: Online Intramural Chess Tournament, of course! Twenty students took to the virtual chessboard in the Online Intramural Chess Tournament, held on March 21 from 7 pm to 8 pm. Judging by their chat activity, the chess players really gave their best and also had quite some fun along the way and it was the chat that really helped bring the experience closer to a real-life chess tournament. We congratulate the organizers and all participants and winners, and are looking forward to more online HanTang student initiatives.

by Carine Fox, 19/08/2018

NEWS FROM THE HANTANG PARENT-TEACHER ASSOCIATION

On October 18, the annual conference of the Parent-Teacher Association (PTA) at HanTang school took place. HanTang president presented the actively engaged parents with an update on the current financial and academic status of the school and together, parents and administration, outlined the activities they want to focus on for the current school year. The members of the Association decided to actively support the construction of a new multi-functional sports field by participating in fundraising and promoting the project. The assembly voted to work on increasing the association’s membership base, to hold monthly working meetings, and to increase the membership fee to 50 euro, effective next school year. “According to the general opinion, the children of parents who are more involved in the learning process, achieve higher academic results, so we can be confident that we have done the maximum for our kids. This association is created by people who believe that helping the whole community to move forward is the best way to establish a launchpad for the individual to jump higher.

by Carine Fox, 19/08/2018

HANTANG WILL HOLD DEBATES OPEN 2019 ON APRIL 13-14

Over the weekend of April 13 - 14 the students from the Debates Club at Hantang will hold the fifth annual consecutive Open Debates Tournament. Sixteen teams from Eindhoven and the country will take part in the competition, which will take place in English. The culmination will be on Sunday, April 14, at the Concert Hall from 12:00 noon to 1:30 PM when the final debate on a prepared topic will take place. The closing & awarding ceremony will follow after, at 2 PM at the same location. The prepared debate topics for the participants are: “This House Believes That: NATO Enhanced Forward Presence is doing more harm than good.”; This House Would ban government funding for art.” and “This House Believes That the Democrats should abandon identity politics as a central campaign tenant.” The tournament will challenge participants to debate impromptu topics for which they will have 1 hour to prepare. Traditionally, the debates tournaments at Hantang attract strong teams from around the country as well as experienced judges. We are expecting a spectacular final round on Sunday, to be attended by alumni, parents, and other members of the Hantang community. We hope for media representatives to also join us and we kindly ask of them to get in touch with us in advance, so that we can facilitate their access to the Hantang building.

by Carine Fox, 19/08/2018
